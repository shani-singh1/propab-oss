The mandrake panel is small: ~56 RT sequences in 7 evolutionary families (≥4 each). Small n is not
a reason to lower the bar — it is a reason to state SHARPER, more conservative, more falsifiable
claims, because an underpowered study confirms noise if you let it. Every hypothesis here must be
scoped to what this sample can decide.

**Discipline for an underpowered panel:**
- **Prefer a directional, single-mechanism claim over a broad one.** With 7 families and few
  sequences per family, a wide multi-feature model overfits and a mean LOFO R² is fragile. Propose
  the narrowest biophysical axis that could still be a real finding, and predict its DIRECTION on a
  named held-out family — a claim a small sample can actually refute.
- **Demand robustness, not just a point estimate.** A hypothesis is only worth testing if it
  survives the family-shuffle null AND a bootstrap interval that does not straddle the family-mean
  baseline. State up front that a signal carried by a single family, or a within-family fit
  swingable by one sequence, does NOT count. Widen your expectations; distrust large effects on
  small subsets.
- **Report the negative honestly.** The correct, common outcome on this panel is that a feature
  tracks family identity and collapses under leave-one-family-out. A hypothesis that names, in
  advance, the family in which it expects to FAIL is stronger than one that expects to win
  everywhere.

**Anti-rediscovery guardrails specific to RT phylogenetics:**
- The RT evolutionary structure is TABULATED and authoritative: the Pfam RVT clan CL0027
  (PF00078 / PF07727 / PF13456), the seven conserved RT catalytic motifs, the ~240-aa domain core,
  and the low (<~25%) cross-class sequence-identity ceiling (Xiong & Eickbush 1990; Pfam 2021). A
  within-family "signal" that is really the known motif conservation, or an apparent signal driven
  by high within-family identity, is a rediscovery or an artifact — never a discovery.
- A claim that a feature "recovers the RT families", "separates the clades", or "matches the known
  RT phylogeny" re-derives established taxonomy. If a feature's whole value is telling families
  apart, it is a lookup against CL0027, not new biology.
- Novelty here means establishing a within/cross-family biophysical predictor of RT ACTIVITY that
  survives both leave-one-family-out and a low-sequence-identity split — ruling out redundancy —
  rather than restating phylogeny. Target that, at a scope the sample can defend.

A good hypothesis on this panel is narrow, directional, names its failure family, commits to the
LOFO + family-shuffle + low-identity test, and would — if it survived — be a genuine cross-family
activity mechanism rather than a re-derivation of the RVT-clan phylogeny.
